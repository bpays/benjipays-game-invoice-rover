The Science

Every mechanic was designed.

Not a game with branding slapped on. Every decision is built around a specific behavioural goal.

📈

Warm-up zone

Vancouver is deliberately easy. Players need to feel competent before difficulty climbs. Competent players come back. Players who die immediately at a booth demo don't.

Game Feel · Retention
💥

Clutch Save

First hit doesn't end the run — resets the combo and grants brief invincibility. The game feels fair. Players who almost died and survived are far more likely to play again.

Fairness · Shareability
🌙

Night mode spike

Power-ups slam the city into night with a music energy drop. This is the moment players describe to the next person in line — and it extends average play length.

Emotion · Run Length
🌆

City as social currency

"I made it to New York" is something to say. Cities give players a concrete achievement to compete over — drives repeat plays and word-of-mouth at the booth.

Narrative · Competition
🎯

Tournament prizes

Daily reset + daily prize + grand prize across 3 days. Yesterday's leader starts fresh every morning. Everyone stays competitive every day.

Retention · Multi-day
🧠

Product metaphors

Benji dodges paper checks and angry clients. Collects ACH transfers and auto-pay badges. Players absorb the brand message without realising they're in a pitch.
